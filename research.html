Research


Diatonic Chord-loops in Post-millennial Pop Harmony

    The prevalent use of diatonic chord-loops in post-millennial pop music reflect the influence of electronic dance music (EDM) that became popular in the mainstream scene around the 2000s. Despite such shift in musical styles in post-millennial pop harmony, there is no centralized harmonic system that can effectively analyze tonal ambiuity, or lack of tonic(s) in chord-loop based songs.

    In my dissertation, I propose a tonic-agnostic system suited for understanding post-millennial pop harmony, which is characterized by cyclic, diatonic chord-loops. In order support my theoretical model, I have been compiling pop harmony data from crowd-sourced chord transcriptions, published corpora from popular music field, and my own transcriptions through close listening.

Mediating Pan-Asian Music through Online Platforms

    As a pre-doctoral research fellow for Platform, Indiana University's global pouplar music research team, I examine the importance of non-physical musical stages that have allowed artists from Asian countries to connect with one another remotely, creating a borderless space for the pan-asian community. I am particularly interested in online platforms such as YouTube and Spotify which not only have changed the way music is consumed today, but also became a platform for protesters of oppressed regime to fight against censorship, sending a message to the global audience.
